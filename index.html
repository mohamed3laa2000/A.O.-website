ABOUT

Hello! I’m Ahmed Osama, a civil engineer and open-water swimmer dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in the water. From the moment I first dipped my toes into a pool, I knew that swimming would be more than just a sport for me—it would be a lifelong journey.

I began swimming at a young age, captivated by the freedom and challenge that the water presented. As the years passed, my dedication to the sport grew stronger, and I honed my skills through relentless training, competing in local and national tournaments, and learning from the best in the field.

ACHIEVEMENTS

- Participated in the world's first relay that combined able-bodied individuals with people of determination to cross the English Channel under the slogan “Able Together” in August 2022, completing it in 12 hours and two minutes.

- Accomplished a solo crossing of the English Channel, swimming non-stop for 14 hours and covering a distance of 65 km in September 2022.

- Secured the 3rd position in the Capri-Napoli International Marathon, covering a distance of 36 km.

- Participated in a relay crossing of the English Channel, including the first wheelchair crossing, in August 2023, completing it in 12 hours and 30 minutes.

- Recognized and honored by the Governor of Port Said in 2022.

- Recognized and honored by the Ministry of Youth and Sport in Egypt for my successful attempt to cross the English Channel in August 2022.

- Recognized and rewarded by President Abdel Fatah El Sisi, President of Egypt, for my outstanding achievements.
